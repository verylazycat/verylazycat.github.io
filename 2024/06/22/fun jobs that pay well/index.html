fun jobs that pay well

Created2024-06-22|Updated2024-07-02
|Word Count:1.6k|Reading Time:9mins|Post Views:

If you score a job that you truly enjoy, it can turn work into play. However, passion alone often isn’t enough to cover expenses or settle student loan debts. So, how can you strike a balance between job satisfaction and a substantial income?

We acknowledge that high-paying roles aren’t always the most enjoyable, which is why we’ve pinpointed positions that are not only enjoyable but also financially rewarding, based on their average salaries.

Art Therapist

Average Salary: $61,101

Blending creativity with psychology, art therapists, after obtaining a master’s degree, can offer their services in educational institutions, psychiatric facilities, and veterans’ organizations, aiding individuals in emotional expression through artistic means.

Despite the rewarding nature of the work, job prospects in this area can be limited, with many states yet to officially recognize art therapy as a licensed profession.

Engineer

Average Salary: $74,000+

The engineering field is diverse, yet united by the common goal of problem-solving across various sectors.

Compensation varies significantly; an agricultural engineer earns a median salary of $74,345, a civil engineer averages $94,490, and an aerospace engineer, involved in rocket and satellite testing, can make $122,970.

Romance Novelist

Average Salary: $43,409

Crafting romantic fiction may appear to be a lucrative vocation, especially when considering the success of “Fifty Shades of Grey” author E.L. James, who boasts a $150 million net worth.

However, earnings for writers can be highly inconsistent. Many up-and-coming romance authors have chosen the path of self-publishing e-books over traditional publishing routes. On Amazon, authors’ annual earnings can range dramatically, from $10,000 to over $1 million.

Cruise Ship Entertainer

Average Salary (for Singers): $57,321

A career on a cruise ship offers the allure of constant travel, with entertainers enjoying unique travel experiences and complimentary accommodations.

Performers, including dancers, magicians, comedians, and musicians, rehearse by day and perform in the evenings. Contracts are typically for three to six months, with weekly pay ranging from $1,500 to $2,500.

Food Scientist

Average Salary: $77,135

Disney Consumer Products’ series, “A Day in the Life of a Food Scientist,” showcases the creative process behind new food product development and flavor experimentation.

While the role may seem entertaining, it’s also demanding, with the majority of positions involving crop experimentation and the assessment of food additives.

Fire Chief

Average Salary: $77,050

Fire chiefs bear significant responsibility, overseeing firefighting crews and operations. The role is not only fulfilling but can also be enjoyable, with some states like New Jersey offering salaries exceeding $96,800 annually.

Music Therapist

Average Salary: $59,917

Music therapists, like their art counterparts, can work in educational, geriatric, and mental health settings, utilizing music to facilitate self-expression and stress reduction.

Lighting Designer

Average Salary: $69,073

For those captivated by concert light shows, a career as a lighting designer could be the ultimate job.

This field typically requires prior experience in school or community theater settings before progressing to higher-paying urban opportunities.

Audio Engineer

Average Salary: $86,391

Audio engineers ensure optimal sound quality in sports arenas, theaters, recording studios, and for TV and film productions.

Opportunities in this entertainment industry-related job are more prevalent in major cities.

Magician

Average Salary: $48,959

While few magicians achieve stardom, it’s still feasible to earn a comfortable income through street performances and private party bookings.

According to CostHelper, private event magicians can earn between $175 and $350 per hour.

LEGO Designer

Average Salary: $64,510

LEGO designers are tasked with creating large-scale sculptures and art installations, with many having backgrounds in industrial design, carpentry, or architecture.

Zookeeper

Average Salary: Up to $40,307

Zookeepers work closely with exotic animals, but the role involves round-the-clock care, often necessitating work on nights, weekends, and holidays.

Personal Trainer

Average Salary: $52,833

For fitness enthusiasts who enjoy motivating others, a career as a personal trainer can be an ideal path.

High-earning trainers, including those working with celebrities, can make significantly more, with the top 10% earning over $73,000 annually.

Stunt Performer

Average Salary: $69,422

Stunt doubles in the film industry are compensated with a daily flat fee, earning at least $1,005 per day, as per the Screen Actors Guild.

Brewmaster

Average Salary: Up to $50,343

Brewmasters are responsible for beer production, including equipment cleaning, recipe testing, and weekend event attendance.

Fashion Designer

Average Salary: $74,157

Fashion designers juggle multiple tasks, from creating clothing lines to managing high-pressure deadlines and making last-minute adjustments for runway models.

Opportunities in this competitive field are often concentrated in major cities.

Homicide Detective

Average Salary: $74,679

For those passionate about solving crimes, a career as a homicide detective can be appealing.

Salaries for criminal investigators can vary greatly, from $42,000 in lower-paying areas to over $130,000 in higher-paying locations.

Winemaker

Average Salary: $89,666

Winemaking is both an art and a science, with winemakers required to have extensive knowledge in food science and chemistry.

The average annual salary for a winemaker is around $86,000, with top earners making close to $132,000, according to Glassdoor.

Concert Promoter

Average Salary: $39,627

Concert promoters collaborate with band managers to secure venues and promote shows, often enjoying perks like complimentary concert tickets.

Airline Pilot

Average Salary: $88,101

Becoming a commercial pilot involves significant time and financial investment in training. The profession can involve working nights and holidays, but the potential for high earnings can be a strong incentive.

Dog Walker

Average Salary: $32,918

Dog walking can be a dream part-time job for animal lovers, with rates ranging from $25 for a 30-minute walk to $35 for 60 minutes, plus additional charges for weekend or holiday services.

Landscape Architect

Average Salary: $97,500

Landscape architects specialize in designing outdoor spaces for parks, businesses, and private residences, utilizing vegetation to create visually appealing environments.

Event Planner

Average Salary: $60,759

Event planners are detail-oriented professionals who coordinate corporate events, conventions, and more, ensuring seamless execution.

Park Ranger

Average Salary: $41,682

Park rangers are responsible for the protection and preservation of national parks, with salaries ranging from $22,000 to $83,000, according to ZipRecruiter.

Astronaut

Average Salary: $65,140

Astronauts embark on extraordinary career paths with the potential for impressive earnings, starting at $65,140 and increasing with experience.

Graphic Designer

Average Salary: $55,650

Graphic designers use a combination of artistic skills and technology to create visual content for advertising, book covers, websites, and more, with many working as self-employed professionals.

Video Game Designer

Average Salary: $71,185

A passion for gaming can translate into a career as a video game designer, though this typically requires a degree in game design, development, or computer science.

Toy Creator

Salary: $59,176

Toy creators play a pivotal role in designing new toys that entertain and educate children, with potential earnings linked to the success of their creations.

Cruise Director

Average Salary: Up to $88,119

Cruise directors are responsible for organizing and overseeing a wide range of activities on cruise ships, with salaries ranging from $38,000 to over $120,000, according to Payscale.com.

Professional Athlete

Average Salary: $53,829

While the median salary reflects a wide range, top athletes can earn millions through contracts and endorsements, whereas some Olympic athletes may earn less than $15,000 annually.

Ethical Computer Hacker

Average Salary: $105,331

Ethical hackers, or information security analysts, use their skills to identify vulnerabilities in web security, helping companies protect against cyber threats.

Wardrobe Stylist

Average Salary: Up to $33,080

Stylists work in retail and as personal consultants, dressing mannequins, creating displays, and assisting clients with fashion choices, with potential earnings ranging from $23,000 to nearly $110,000 a year.

Personal Shopper

Average Salary: Up to $32,281

Personal shoppers offer a valuable service to busy individuals, handling their shopping needs and earning between $30,000 and $60,000 a year.

Marine Biologist

Average Salary: $43,911

Marine biologists dedicate their careers to the study and care of marine life, typically requiring a bachelor’s degree and sometimes a master’s for more advanced roles.

Interior Designer

Average Salary: $66,607

Interior designers create aesthetic and functional spaces, requiring knowledge of design principles as well as building codes and regulations.

Chocolatier

Average Salary: $32,668

Chocolatiers specialize in crafting chocolate confections, with potential earnings reaching up to $100,000 a year for those working with prestigious brands like Godiva.

Supercar Driving Instructor

Average Salary: $120,000

Instructing owners of luxury high-performance cars can be an exhilarating career, with some instructors reportedly earning up to $120,000 a year.

Restaurant Critic

Average Salary: $54,475

Restaurant critics share their culinary experiences and opinions, with top critics potentially earning up to $92,461 a year.

Professional Video Gamer

Average Salary: $56,202

Professional gamers can earn a living through live performances, video platforms, and eSport competitions, with top earners making six figures annually.
