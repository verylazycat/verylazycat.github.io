what forex pairs move the most

Created2024-07-07|Updated2024-07-07
|Word Count:971|Reading Time:6mins|Post Views:

Key Insights

Forex currency pairs display different levels of volatility, affecting their attractiveness for trading. Major pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY are the most liquid due to high trading volumes and typically offer smaller bid/ask spreads, creating more favorable trading environments.

When identifying which pairs experience the most daily pip movements, consider economic news, geopolitical events, and technical analysis tools such as chart patterns and moving averages. AUD/USD and NZD/USD are examples of pairs that are highly mobile and suitable for trend-following strategies, while AUD/JPY and NZD/JPY are preferred for carry trade strategies.

Active Forex Pairs

High volatility forex pairs are considered the most active, providing ample opportunities for traders to utilize their analysis and strategies. Key pairs to monitor include EUR/USD, USD/JPY, and GBP/USD:

  • EUR/USD is the most traded pair, influenced by global economic events and news, offering frequent fluctuations.
  • USD/JPY is favored for its stability and moderate fluctuations, making it a reliable choice.
  • GBP/USD is highly volatile, influenced by news and political events, appealing to traders with a taste for high-risk, high-reward scenarios.

Staying informed on market trends and news is crucial for anticipating forex pair movements. Strategic trading and technical analysis are essential for identifying trends and maximizing profits in the dynamic forex market.

Major Pairs

  • EUR/USD is ideal for tracking currency movements.
  • USD/JPY is suited for news trading.
  • GBP/USD is appropriate for technical analysis and chart pattern identification.
  • USD/CHF is good for breakout and range trading.
  • AUD/USD is fitting for scalping and swing strategies.
  • NZD/USD is beneficial for risk management and leverage.
  • USD/CAD is used for analyzing bid/ask spreads and interest rates.

EUR/USD

Known as the Euro to US Dollar pair, EUR/USD is a top forex market contender, representing the exchange rate between the Euro and the US Dollar. It’s influenced by economic and political factors of both regions and is used as a hedge by investors.

USD/JPY

Globally traded, USD/JPY reflects the exchange rate between the US Dollar and Japanese Yen. It’s highly influenced by economic news from both countries and is popular for news trading and technical analysis.

GBP/USD

The British pound sterling against the US dollar, or GBP/USD, is known for high volatility. It’s heavily influenced by news and political events, making it a popular choice for risk-seeking traders.

USD/CHF

Referred to as “the Swissy,” USD/CHF represents the US dollar against the Swiss franc. It’s known for low volatility range trading and is popular among technical analysts.

AUD/USD

The AUD/USD pair is highly active, attracting traders with its volatility and trend-following nature. It’s popular for various trading strategies and is influenced by commodity prices due to Australia’s role as a leading commodity exporter.

NZD/USD

The NZD/USD pair is liquid and volatile, offering opportunities for risk management. It’s affected by economic data releases, geopolitical events, and interest rate changes.

USD/CAD

The USD/CAD pair is active, influenced by factors such as bid/ask spread, session overlaps, and central bank decisions. It’s also impacted by interest rates and crude oil demand, given Canada’s role as a significant oil exporter.

Crosses

Forex crosses like EUR/JPY, GBP/JPY, AUD/JPY, NZD/JPY, EUR/GBP, EUR/CHF, GBP/CHF, and CHF/JPY offer unique opportunities based on their volatility and the economic factors of the regions they represent.

EUR/JPY

The EUR/JPY cross pair is sensitive to geopolitical events and economic indicators from both Europe and Japan, making it a popular choice for traders monitoring global economic activity.

GBP/JPY

Highly volatile and liquid, GBP/JPY is influenced by consumer sentiment, oil and gold prices, and the unpredictability of commodity currencies.

AUD/JPY

The AUD/JPY cross pair is known for significant volatility and opportunities for carry trade strategies, with traders advised to set stop-loss and take-profit levels due to the risks involved.

NZD/JPY

The NZD/JPY pair requires careful risk management and discipline, with traders encouraged to backtest strategies using historical data for better insights.

EUR/GBP

The EUR/GBP pair is characterized by high liquidity and volatility, with automated trading systems often employed to capitalize on its consistent movement.

EUR/CHF

The EUR/CHF pair is known for its lower volatility and small bid-ask spreads, influenced by demand for long-duration assets from Swiss banks.

GBP/CHF

The GBP/CHF pair is moderately volatile, with an average daily pip movement of 77, and is influenced by geopolitical events affecting both the UK and Switzerland.

CHF/JPY

The CHF/JPY cross is active in the forex market, with an average daily pip movement of 60-80, influenced by global economic and political changes.

Forex Pairs Moving the Most Pips

  • The EUR/USD pair moves the most pips daily, averaging about 100 pips.
  • Other active pairs include USD/JPY, GBP/USD, AUD/USD, and USD/CHF, with daily ranges of 50-80 pips.
  • Forex pairs with emerging market currencies tend to have higher volatility.
  • Economic data releases and geopolitical events significantly impact forex volatility.
  • Technical analysis tools are widely used to identify potential price movements.

FAQs

  • The most pip-moving forex pairs are EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CHF, AUD/USD, and USD/CAD.
  • A pip is the smallest unit of change in forex trading.
  • Forex pairs with currencies from major economies tend to move more pips due to higher liquidity.
  • Pip movement tracking can be done using a charting platform with a pips-based price format.
  • The pip movement significance lies in indicating volatility and trading opportunities.
  • Forex pairs moving fewer pips can still be profitable with appropriate strategies and risk management.
